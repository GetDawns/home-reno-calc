# How much paint do I need for a 12x14 room?

**Short answer:** A standard 12x14 room with 8-foot ceilings, one door, and two windows needs about **2 gallons of paint for two coats** (or 3 gallons if you're also painting the ceiling). [Use our paint calculator](/calculators/paint-calculator) to get exact numbers for your specific room.

Now the long answer — including the math, why buying just enough is a mistake, and the four variables that change everything.

## The exact math for a 12x14 room

Walls in a rectangular room are calculated using the perimeter, not the area:

- **Perimeter:** 2 × (12 + 14) = **52 feet**
- **Wall area (8 ft ceilings):** 52 × 8 = **416 sq ft**
- **Subtract one door (~21 sq ft) + two windows (~30 sq ft):** 416 − 51 = **365 sq ft of paintable wall**

A gallon of standard interior paint covers ~350 sq ft per coat on smooth, primed walls. For two coats with a 10% buffer:

- 365 sq ft × 2 coats × 1.10 = **803 sq ft of paint coverage needed**
- 803 ÷ 350 = **2.3 gallons**

So you'll need 2 gallons plus a quart, or just round up to 3 gallons if you want extra for touch-ups.

## What changes the answer

### 1. Wall height

Most homes are 8 ft, but newer construction often uses 9 or 10 ft ceilings. A 12x14 room with 9-ft ceilings has 468 sq ft of wall area — about 28% more paint.

### 2. Doors and windows

Each standard door deducts ~21 sq ft. Each standard window deducts ~15 sq ft. A room with three big windows and two doors has noticeably less wall to paint than one with a single window.

### 3. Including the ceiling

A 12x14 ceiling adds 168 sq ft. With 2 coats and the 10% buffer, that's about another half gallon — so you'd need 3 gallons total instead of 2.

> **Pro tip:** Ceilings usually need flat-finish *ceiling paint*, not the same paint as your walls. They're formulated to flatten light reflection and minimize roller-spatter from above-shoulder painting.

### 4. Texture and surface type

Smooth, primed drywall takes the rated 350 sq ft per gallon. Textured walls (orange peel, knockdown) drop to about 275 sq ft. New, unprimed drywall is even thirstier — sometimes 200 sq ft for the first coat as the paper soaks up the binder.

## Why "just enough" is the wrong target

Three reasons to buy a quart extra beyond what the calculator says:

1. **Touch-ups.** You will scuff a wall. A nail will go in the wrong spot. The dog will brush against trim with wet paint. Save a quart in a tightly sealed jar for a year.
2. **Color drift.** Mixed-to-order colors can vary slightly between batches. Buying the same color months later may not perfectly match.
3. **Hidden coverage problems.** Going light-over-dark, painting fresh drywall, or using cheaper paint can all increase how much paint each coat absorbs. The 10% buffer in our [paint calculator](/calculators/paint-calculator) accounts for this — but on tricky projects, plan for 15%.

## What about primer?

Most modern paints are paint-and-primer combos — they include a binder strong enough to bond to previously painted walls in good condition. You only need separate primer when:

- Painting fresh drywall (the paper soaks up regular paint badly)
- Going light-over-dark (red walls especially) where you want to avoid 3+ coats
- Covering stains (water rings, smoke, marker) that would bleed through paint
- Switching from oil-based to latex paint

A gallon of primer covers about the same area as a gallon of paint, so plan accordingly.

## Step-by-step: estimating your specific room

1. **Measure floor length and width** in feet. Note your wall height (most likely 8 ft).
2. **Count doors and windows** in the room. A regular bedroom door is one; a closet sliding door pair counts as two.
3. **Decide if the ceiling is included.** If yes, plan for an extra half-gallon.
4. **Plug it into our [paint calculator](/calculators/paint-calculator)** for the exact gallons (and quart, if applicable).
5. **Add a quart of overage** for touch-ups, regardless of what the calculator says.

## What this room actually costs

At about $35 per gallon for mid-tier interior paint (Behr Premium Plus, Valspar Reserve, etc.), a 12x14 room runs:

- **Walls only, 2 coats:** ~$70–$100 in paint
- **Walls + ceiling, 2 coats:** ~$100–$130 in paint
- **Premium paint (Behr Marquee, Sherwin-Williams Emerald):** add ~$15–$25 per gallon

That's before brushes, rollers, drop cloths, painter's tape, and trim paint. Budget another $40–$80 for tools if you don't already own them.

## Frequently asked questions

**How long does 2 gallons of paint last on a 12x14 room?**
A 2-gallon job typically takes 4–6 hours for one person, plus drying time between coats (usually 2–4 hours). Plan a full day end-to-end.

**Do I need to thin the paint?**
For brush and roller, no. For sprayers, sometimes — check your sprayer's manual.

**How long does paint last in the can?**
Latex paint stored in a sealed can in a cool, dry place lasts 2–10 years. Look for thick layers, foul smell, or chunks before reuse.
